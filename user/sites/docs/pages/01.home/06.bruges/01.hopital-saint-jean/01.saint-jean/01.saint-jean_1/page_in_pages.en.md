! The triptych has been conceived and realised for the master altar of the new apse of the church of the Old St. John Hospital of Bruges (in Flemish: Brugge) built in 1473-74, where it still stands.  
! The frame bears the inscription « {r=la/fr}WORK(OPVs)&#160;()OF&#160;HANS(IOHANNIS)&#160;()MEMLING(MEMLING){/r} », the first known signature of the flemish primitive painter of Bruges Hans MEMLING, and also the date: «1479».  
! It has a central panel, framed by two panels on the right and left fixed by hinges.   
! The characters depicted are a little smaller than life size.  
! these fews pages aim at presenting its different aspects to allow a deeper understanding:
! 1. first, by a photographic presentation of the triptych in its exhibition space,
! 2. then thrue a description of what it represents,
! 3. finally by relating the different elements of the image with the different texts at the origin of the represented scenes.

<br>

## about this article {.orange #a-propos-de-cet-article}

To produce this article, we are indebted to reference works, particularly those of [Dirk DE VOS, “Hans Memling - The Complete Work”](#de-vos-1){.orange #renvoi-de-vos-1} and [“Hans Memling”](#de-vos-2){.orange #renvoi-de-vos-2}.  
However, our aim is not to produce an interpretation of the work here, but rather [color=#e6a64d]a description[/color] and through it to make available the elements that make it possible to grasp it: [color=#e6a64d]quality reproductions[/color] [<sup style="color:#555;">1</sup>](#note-1 "note-1"){#renvoi-note-1} , [color=#e6a64d]the main sources of the work[/color], whether textual or pictorial, which today, even in the case of specialised paper works available, for example, in libraries, is too often done only by reference or implied.  
Our goal will have been achieved if the reader is in a position to [color=#e6a64d]measure and thus deepen his or her own perception[/color] of a work that we think deserves attention [color=#e6a64d]as much as is great the radiance of its presence[/color].
